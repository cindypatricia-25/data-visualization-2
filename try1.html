Road Crashes and Safety Insights Across Australia

Using data from the Australian Government, Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts, spanning from 2019 to 2023, we have created meaningful visualizations to highlight road safety across Australia. The visualization particularly emphasizes the higher number of road crashes in New South Wales compared to other states, showcasing the importance of addressing safety measures in this region.

Geographic and Environmental Insights

Crash by Local Government Area (LGA)

This map illustrates the distribution of road crashes across Australia at the Local Government Area (LGA) level. Each region is color-coded based on the number of reported crashes, with darker shades representing higher crash counts. The white areas on the map indicate LGAs where no road crashes were detected during the period of analysis. This visualization provides a geographic overview of road safety, helping identify regions with higher incidences of crashes. By highlighting crash hotspots, the map supports data-driven decision-making, allowing local authorities and policy-makers to focus on regions that may benefit from targeted road safety interventions.

FUN FACT! The central region of Australia is largely uninhabitable due to receiving less than 500 mm of rain annually.

Crash Count by Remoteness

In general, the less remote an area is, the higher the number of crashes. Exercise extra caution when driving on busy roads!

Speed Limit and Crash Incidence

The lower the speed limit, the fewer crashes—indicated by smaller bubbles. Be cautious when driving, as higher speeds increase stopping distance!
